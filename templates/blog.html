Understanding Breast Cancer: What You Need to Know

Breast cancer affects millions of women worldwide each year. Understanding the basics can help you make informed decisions about your health and support loved ones through their journey.

Breast cancer occurs when cells in the breast tissue begin to grow uncontrollably. These cells can form a tumor that may be felt as a lump or seen on an X-ray. While breast cancer primarily affects women, men can also develop this disease.

Key Risk Factors:
  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • Family history of breast cancer
  • Genetic mutations (BRCA1, BRCA2)
  • Personal history of breast conditions
  • Lifestyle factors (alcohol, obesity, lack of exercise)
Did you know?
Early detection can increase 5-year survival rates to over 90%.

Prevention Strategies for Breast Cancer

While not all breast cancers can be prevented, there are several strategies that can help reduce your risk:

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Exercise regularly (150 minutes/week)
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Breastfeed if possible
  • Regular screening and self-exams

Understanding Mammograms and Screenings

Regular screening is crucial for early detection. Here's what you need to know:

  • Mammograms detect tumors before they're palpable
  • Recommended starting age: 40-50 years
  • Annual or biennial screening based on risk
  • 3D mammography provides better visualization
  • False positives are common but manageable
